About the Project


Audience

We address our project to:

  • scholars – historians or linguists, who make research about texts or life in that time
  • people curious about history
  • occasional users, browsing the Internet (who actually may be the majority of our audience)

Goals

  • To unite manuscripts from opposite sides and publish a diplomatic edition of the letters we found, provide different points of view on the First World War
  • To tell stories of real people from that period, soldiers and army officers make these themes more readable for ordinary people
  • To provide all texts in three of our languages (Italian, Turkish, Russian) and also in English to allow people from different countries to read all texts and make their suggestions
  • To examine lexis that were used to describe the war and compare texts written by military opponents

Methodology

We have found six heroes from different sides of the barricades:

  • Russian Peasant
  • Latvian Teacher
  • Turkish Teacher and Lieutenant

We collected manuscripts that were written by these people. Now we have observed 11 letters and diaries from the World War I period that are in Italian, Russian, and Turkish languages. To find these documents we checked several digital and offline libraries in Russia, Turkey, and European Union.

We have made online research and also contacted with some Turkish universities and archives – the archive of the Ministry of National Defense of Turkey, Uludağ University History Department. We analyzed the used language. We analyzed not only original letters but also made a comparison of versions in English to understand the “mood” of the letters and common lemmas. In our analysis, we concentrated mostly on things that could be interesting for a wide audience, because our main goal – is to show how the war is described by ordinary internet users. Thus, we observed the lexis, the meaning of the words, how the language was changed, etc. For a clear understanding of that situation, we are providing a map with marks all places where were created all manuscripts and information about the historical background and also all facts which we have found about the authors of the letters.
