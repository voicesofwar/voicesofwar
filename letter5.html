Memoir Letters of Mehmet Dursun on the Battle of Canakkale

Lieutenant Mehmet Dursun fought and lost an arm in the battle of Çanakkale. The letter from him is written in 1915.

Analysis

The grammar used by Umberto is perfect as is his use of punctuation. In the last letter he uses grand terms to address how he scaled the ranks of the army. He mentions that he wants to be transferred either to Somalia or Eritrea, even asking for a recommendation from the ministry of war, this is a clear sign that his family came from a well-known family that had contacts in the ministry. The letters of Umberto show a completely different perspective on the war, if we look at the letters of Michele we can see for starters that from the way he writes he is not a person that has studied and also, from the information that we can find in the text, he doesn’t have the opportunity to be shipped, since he is a simple soldier.

Before the Letter revolution in 1928, Turkish language was written using Arabic letters. But after 1928, Latin alphabet was introduced and used solely. So here we see the Latin transcription which is also how Turkish is written right now.
Also this letter wasn’t written to a specific person, it is rather a memoir of the battle time in the author’s life. As a consequence we don’t see lots of emotional words or words for addressing people. The author tells a story, he uses a lot of military related words related to the war period he lived in. When we look at the war-specific content and usage of words we see some similarly used words between two letters.
- Asker (soldier)
- Düşman (enemy)
- Süngü (bayonet)
If we look at the letters individually, there are many more used words, some examples are:
- Tabur (battalion)
- Bölük (company)
- Alay (regiment)
- Kurşun (bullet)
- Cephe (front)
- Taarruz (attack)
