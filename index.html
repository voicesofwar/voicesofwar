WELCOME TO THE VOICES OF WAR

Welcome to the Voices of War, where history come to life through the people that lived it. Immerse yourself in the experiences of our heroes as you browse our collection of letters from the Great War. From the trenches to the home front, these letters offer a new perspective on the events that shaped the world. This website offers a unique and intimate look at one of the most devastating events in human history through the words of those who lived it. These letters provide a glimpse into the hopes, fears, and experiences of those whose lives were changed by the war. Whether you’re a history enthusiast or simply curious about this era, we invite you to take a journey back in time and explore the heartfelt and moving correspondence of those who were part of the Great War.

HISTORICAL BACKGROUND

The beginning of World War 1 was marked by a complex web of treaties and alliances between the world's major powers. It started in 1914 with the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria-Hungary, which led to a series of events that eventually led to the declaration of war. On July 28, 1914, Austria-Hungary declared war on Serbia, setting off a chain reaction of events that quickly escalated into a full-scale global conflict. The war was fought between the Central Powers (led by Germany, Austria-Hungary, and the Ottoman Empire) and the Allied Powers (led by France, the United Kingdom, and Russia). Initially, Italy was part of the Triple Alliance with Germany and Austria-Hungary, but ultimately decided to remain neutral at the outbreak of war. In 1915, Italy switched sides and joined the Allied Powers after secret negotiations promised them territorial gains in exchange for their involvement in the war.
