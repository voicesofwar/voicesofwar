What did the letters tell us?

After conducting a thorough analysis of English translations of letters we had, we have observed that a few words appear frequently in the translations. The most common words used are "Hope," "Dear," "My," and "Mother." These words seem to be recurrent across different texts, suggesting that they hold a significant meaning and impact on the content being conveyed. As these letters were mostly sent to family members around the most terrifying times, the usage of sentimental words are expected.
