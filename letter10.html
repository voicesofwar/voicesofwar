Analysis

After the collapse of the monarchy in 1917 in Russia, Soviet government conducted the reform of the language and grammar to make writing more simple for a wider audience. They wanted to educate people and make reading and writing much more reachable for workers and the peasants also. We found the information that after this reform, armed men were sent to the printing houses to remove the cancelled letters specifically to prevent even the technical possibility of printing according to the old standards. In observed texts we dealt with the old grammar rules and letters which do not exist in the contemporary Russian language (І, Ъ, Ѣ, etc.). The share of these words is quite significant.

Observing two people's writing style, we could compare their grammar level. For example, letters of Jānis Fārts are written almost without any errors (we have found two but it could be indications of authorial style). On the contrary, diary of the farmer Ivan Kucherenko contains many errors, but considering that he is a farmer, he also presented good spelling.
