Analysis

Before the Letter revolution in 1928, Turkish language was written using Arabic letters. But after 1928, Latin alphabet was introduced and used solely. So here we see the Latin transcription which is also how Turkish is written right now. There are lots of words for emotions because it is written to author’s mother. We see the language usage is more sentimental. We can see that there are some types of words that are rarely used in contemporary modern Turkish. We can also realize that some words are not written as they were at those times.

Effects of Religion

In those times another aspect is the effect of religion in letters. The majority of the men sent to war were Muslims and they most of the time included prayings in the letters as well. For example in the letter of Martyr Teacher Hasan Ethem Efendi to His Mother, there is a whole paragraph of prayer: "O great god of the Turks! O you, this bird who sings, that wandering and bleating sheep, that prostrate green crop and grass, those majestic mountains! You gave all these to the Turks. Leave it to the Turks again; Because such beautiful places, is reserved for the Turks who bless and recognize the greatness of you.” "O my Lord! All the wishes of these heroic soldiers are to introduce your name to the British and the French. Grant this honorable wish and sharpen the bayonets of us soldiers who pray to you in such a beautiful and quiet place, trembling in your presence. You have already destroyed our enemies, destroy them completely!” Of course in the original text as we can see, the “Lord” is written as “Allah”. Grammar

The errors we have highlighted were not considered as errors at the time. As Turkish language evolved through time, some grammar rules have also changed. Examples:
- Hissettirmeğe => hissettirmeye
- Vâlideciğim => valideciğim
- Mektub => mektup
There are also some errors that are still considered errors. Examples:
- Bulunduğumdan => bulunduğuma
- Nasihatimiz => nasihatiniz
- Dalma => dalına
